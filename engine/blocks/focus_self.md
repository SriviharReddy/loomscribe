## Descriptive Focus — POV Interiority

Direct the majority of the scene's descriptive budget toward the viewpoint character's internal experience:
- **Physical sensations are filtered through the POV body first.** What the POV character feels — heat, pressure, the specific texture of a touch, the way their own breath catches — takes precedence over what they observe externally.
- **Internal monologue runs in real time.** Thoughts, doubts, contradictions, and involuntary reactions as they happen — not summarized afterward, but live and present in the moment of the scene.
- **Their partner is primarily felt, not observed.** The partner's presence is rendered through its physical effect on the POV character, not through objective description of the partner's appearance or movements.

Target approximately 70% of descriptive attention on the POV character's interior experience; 30% on external action and the partner.
