## Descriptive Focus — Balanced

Distribute the scene's descriptive attention evenly between the POV character's internal experience and the partner's visible responses:
- **Alternate between inward and outward.** Move between the POV character's sensations and the partner's reactions in deliberate rhythm — following the natural pulse of the scene, not randomly.
- **Neither channel dominates.** The POV character's interiority receives equal weight to their close observation of the partner. Both are alive in the scene simultaneously.
- **Transitions are deliberate.** The shift from internal to external (and back) should follow what the scene demands — a beat of the POV character's internal conflict, then a close read of the partner's face, then back.

Target approximately 50/50 between the POV character's internal experience and the partner's observable responses.
