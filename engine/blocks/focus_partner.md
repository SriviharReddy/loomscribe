## Descriptive Focus — Partner Reaction

Direct the majority of the scene's descriptive budget toward the partner's visible and audible responses:
- **Watch the partner closely.** Their face, body, voice, and involuntary responses are the primary channel. What their eyes are doing. How their breathing changes. Where the tension lives in their body and how it shifts.
- **Make responses specific, not generic.** "Gasped" and "moaned" are placeholders. Write the specific quality of the sound, the specific thing their body did, the specific expression that crossed their face and what it revealed.
- **The POV character reads the partner.** The scene is experienced through the POV character's attentive observation of the partner's state — desire, conflict, surrender, resistance — as it changes moment to moment.

Target approximately 70% of descriptive attention on the partner's responses; 30% on the POV character's own actions and sensations.
